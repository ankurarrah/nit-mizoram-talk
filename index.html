13
Round by Round

The Interview Playbook

Round 0 Online Assessment

2-3 coding problems in 60-90 min. Difficulty: LeetCode Easy-Medium.

Prep: Solve 200+ LeetCode. Focus on arrays, strings, trees, graphs, DP.

Pass rate: ~30% of applicants clear this stage.

Rounds 1-2 DSA Deep Dive

45-60 min each. One problem, go deep. Interviewer watches you think.

Pattern: Clarify → Brute force → Optimize → Code → Test

Key topics: Trees, graphs, sliding window, binary search, DP

Round 3 System Design (Lite)

For new grads: simpler scope. Design a URL shortener, chat app, or parking lot.

Know: Client-server, REST APIs, databases (SQL vs NoSQL), caching basics

Resource: Gaurav Sen YouTube, Alex Xu's System Design book

Round 4 Behavioral / Hiring Manager

Culture fit + past experiences. Uses the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Prep: 5 stories ready: a hard bug, team conflict, leadership, failure, proud moment

Microsoft cares about: Growth mindset, collaboration, customer obsession

15
Let's Clear This Up

Myths Busted

"Only IIT/top NIT students get into FAANG"

Microsoft hires from 100+ colleges in India. Google's hiring page says "we don't care where you studied." Off-campus hiring doesn't even ask your college name in the OA.

TRUTH: Your skills clear interviews, not your college name.

"CGPA doesn't matter at all"

Some companies have cutoffs (6.5-7.0). Many recruiters glance at it as a first filter. After your first job, it never comes up again.

TRUTH: Keep 8+ to be safe. It's a hygiene factor, not a differentiator.

"I need to know every technology"

You don't need React + Django + ML + Blockchain + Cloud. That's breadth without depth. Interviewers spot this instantly.

TRUTH: Master 1 language, 1 stack. Go deep. Depth > Breadth always.

"People who get in are just lucky or connected"

Luck is real. But luck favors the prepared. The person who solved 500 LeetCode problems and applied to 50 companies will "get lucky" more often.

TRUTH: Luck = preparation x opportunity x persistence.

Beyond Skills

The Mindset That Wins

Skills get you the interview. Mindset gets you the career.

01

Be Proactive

Don't wait for assignments, placements, or permission. The best opportunities go to people who move first.

  • ▸ Learn tech before your course covers it
  • ▸ Message engineers on LinkedIn — ask questions
  • ▸ Volunteer for hard tasks nobody wants
  • ▸ Start the coding club if one doesn't exist
"I learned React before my team adopted it. When they needed someone, I was ready. That's not luck — that's positioning."
02

Be Consistent

Motivation fades. Discipline stays. The difference between "talented" and "successful" is showing up on day 187.

  • ▸ 45 min/day × 240 days = 180 hours
  • ▸ That's a full college course, self-taught
  • ▸ Track your streak — don't break the chain
  • ▸ Small daily > heroic weekly
"I solved LeetCode every morning for 8 months while working full-time. Not exciting. Not viral. But it got me into Microsoft."
03

Work Hard

There's no hack, no shortcut, no secret. The people at the top outworked everyone when it mattered.

  • ▸ Late nights debugging are part of the job
  • ▸ Weekend projects build your portfolio
  • ▸ Preparing while others are Netflix-ing
  • ▸ Talent is overrated. Grit is underrated.
"Every senior engineer I admire has stories of weekends spent learning, failing, rebuilding. There is no shortcut past the work."
